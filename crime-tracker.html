Important context: Per-capita rates are only shown for nationalities with a valid asylum system denominator (people on Home Office support or awaiting a decision). EU/EEA nationals (Polish, Romanian, etc.) and other non-asylum populations are shown as prison counts only, because their UK population isn't captured by asylum data. The Migration Observatory has shown that once you adjust for age and sex demographics, non-citizens are actually under-represented in the prison population.

Note: The MOJ publishes prison offence breakdowns for foreign nationals as a group, not by individual nationality. For conviction data broken down by individual nationality, see the Convictions by Nationality tab. Drug offences and miscellaneous crimes (immigration offences) are notably over-represented among foreign nationals; violence and sexual offences are under-represented relative to British nationals in the prison population.

Key limitation: The MOJ does not publish prison population offence breakdowns by individual nationality - only by nationality group (British / Foreign National / Not Recorded).
